# DeepSeek Changelog

## 2026-06

### DeepSeek API and model updates
DeepSeek has continued to refine its V3 general-purpose model and R1 reasoning model family. The API remains one of the lowest-cost options for high-quality language modeling, with particular strength in Chinese and English mixed-language tasks.

### Open-weight ecosystem
DeepSeek's open-weight releases continue to drive community fine-tuning, quantization, and local deployment. The models are widely available on Ollama, vLLM, LM Studio, and other local inference platforms.

## 2026-05

### Reasoning model improvements
R1-class models received incremental improvements in math, code, and long-context reasoning. DeepSeek has been competitive with OpenAI's o-series on several reasoning benchmarks at a fraction of the price.

## What this means for users

DeepSeek is the price-disruption leader. If cost is your primary constraint, DeepSeek is almost always worth testing. The trade-offs are typically latency, availability in your region, and the level of enterprise support. For experimentation and internal tools, it is hard to beat on value.

## What to watch

- API reliability and regional availability.
- Whether DeepSeek releases a next-generation V4 or R2.
- Independent evaluations of reasoning quality versus OpenAI o-series and Kimi K2.
