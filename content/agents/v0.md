## When to choose v0

Choose v0 when your project is frontend-heavy and already lives in the Vercel ecosystem. It is the fastest way to generate a shippable React/Next.js interface from a description, an image, or an existing design system.

## What it does well

- **UI-first generation.** v0 understands components, layouts, spacing, and Tailwind classes. The output looks like a real product, not a wireframe.
- **Design system imports.** Bring in shadcn/ui, your own component library, or Figma designs to keep generated code consistent with the rest of your product.
- **One-click deploy.** Generated Next.js apps can be pushed straight to Vercel, turning a prompt into a live URL in seconds.
- **Iterative chat refinement.** You can ask for adjustments — "make the hero darker," "add a pricing section" — and the UI updates.
- **Full-stack Next.js support.** Beyond pure UI, v0 can scaffold pages with server logic, routes, and data fetching.

## Honest limitations

- **Vercel-centric.** The best experience is tied to Vercel hosting and Next.js. Other stacks get less love.
- **Not a backend builder.** While it handles Next.js server code, deep backend architecture, databases, and DevOps are outside its sweet spot.
- **Limited model control.** v0 chooses and routes models; you do not bring your own key.
- **Scope ceiling.** Excellent for landing pages, dashboards, and components. For sprawling applications, you will outgrow the prompt workflow.

## Best fit

Frontend engineers, founders, and designers who want to ship Next.js interfaces fast and are happy to stay inside the Vercel ecosystem.
