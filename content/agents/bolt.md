## When to choose Bolt.new

Choose Bolt when you have an app idea and want to be looking at a working URL within minutes, not days. It is the fastest way to move from "what if?" to a deployed full-stack prototype with database, auth, and frontend in place.

## What it does well

- **End-to-end generation.** A single prompt can produce a React frontend, Node backend, data models, and auth wiring. You do not have to glue the layers together.
- **Built-in deployment.** Hosting, databases, and analytics are included. There is no separate Vercel or Supabase setup step.
- **Import and export.** Bring in Figma designs or GitHub repos, then push the finished work back out to GitHub when you are ready for production tooling.
- **Rapid iteration.** You can keep refining through chat: change the UI, add a feature, fix a bug — the agent applies the change across the whole app.
- **Browser-based workspace.** No local install required. It runs on StackBlitz's WebContainer technology entirely in the browser tab.

## Honest limitations

- **Cloud lock-in.** The fastest path runs on StackBlitz infrastructure. You can export, but the native hosting and database are proprietary.
- **Code quality varies.** Prototypes ship fast; production refactoring is still on you. Complex apps can accumulate architectural shortcuts.
- **Limited model control.** You use the models Bolt selects. There is no BYO-key or local-model fallback.
- **Not for large codebases.** It shines on greenfield MVPs, not on joining a team with years of existing code.

## Best fit

Founders, designers, and indie hackers who need to validate ideas or build small production apps quickly. Best treated as a fast prototype-to-GitHub workflow rather than a long-term production host.
