## When to choose Microsoft Scout

Choose Scout when you want an agent that lives in the background of a Microsoft-centric workplace and can act across desktop apps, files, and cloud services. It is designed for enterprises that value governance over raw autonomy.

## What it does well

- **Cross-app desktop automation.** Scout can move between the file system, browser, Teams, Outlook, and SharePoint as if it were an employee sitting at the keyboard.
- **Microsoft 365 native.** It understands Entra identity, SharePoint permissions, Outlook calendars, and Teams conversations without custom connectors.
- **Approval gates.** Consequential actions pause for human approval before executing, which makes it palatable to security and compliance teams.
- **Governance integration.** Purview and Entra logging mean actions are auditable. This is the key differentiator over consumer-grade agents.
- **Always-on background operation.** Unlike chat assistants that wait for a prompt, Scout can proactively monitor and act on workflows.

## Honest limitations

- **Microsoft lock-in.** The full experience requires Entra, Microsoft 365, and Windows 11 or macOS. Organizations outside that stack need not apply.
- **Limited availability.** Scout is rolling out through the Microsoft Frontier early-access program. Pricing and availability are not yet settled.
- **Closed and opaque.** You cannot inspect the models, the planning logic, or the data handling. Enterprise trust is the selling point, not transparency.
- **Potential for disruption.** An always-on agent touching email, files, and calendars needs careful policy tuning before wide deployment.

## Best fit

Microsoft 365-centric enterprises that need a governed, auditable agent for cross-app automation and are willing to accept Microsoft's closed ecosystem in exchange for centralized control.
