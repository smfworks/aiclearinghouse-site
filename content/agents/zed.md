## When to choose Zed

Choose Zed when performance and openness matter more than ecosystem polish. It is the fastest, most privacy-respecting option for developers who want a modern editor with AI that does not phone home to a proprietary model provider.

## What it does well

- **Raw speed.** Rust + GPU acceleration makes Zed feel instantaneous on large files and projects. It is noticeably faster than Electron-based editors.
- **Open source core.** The editor is free and the source is open. You can inspect, fork, and self-build.
- **Bring-your-own-key AI.** Plug in your own OpenAI, Anthropic, or other API keys. Your code and spend stay under your control.
- **Open-weight edit prediction.** Zeta2.1 predicts edits locally when possible, reducing latency and cost.
- **Multiplayer editing.** Shared buffers and voice channels make it a genuine pair-programming environment.
- **Clean, minimal UI.** The interface gets out of the way. If you disliked VS Code's density, Zed is a breath of fresh air.

## Honest limitations

- **Smaller extension market.** The ecosystem is growing but still behind VS Code. Some niche tools are missing.
- **Learning curve for Vim/VS Code migrants.** Keybindings and workflows are different enough that switching takes intentional effort.
- **AI features less mature than Cursor.** Agentic workflows exist but are not as deeply integrated as dedicated AI-first IDEs.
- **Collaboration requires buy-in.** Multiplayer only shines if teammates also use Zed.

## Best fit

Performance-sensitive developers, open-source purists, and teams that want a fast, privacy-respecting editor with optional BYO-key AI and real-time collaboration.
