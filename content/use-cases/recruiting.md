# Recruiting Agents

## What they do

Recruiting agents automate the high-volume, repetitive parts of hiring: sourcing candidates, writing job descriptions, screening resumes, scheduling interviews, and personalizing outreach. They let recruiters spend more time on relationships and decisions.

## Common tasks

- **Job description drafting.** Generate clear, inclusive job posts from role requirements.
- **Sourcing.** Find candidates from LinkedIn, GitHub, and other public profiles.
- **Resume screening.** Compare applicants against must-have and nice-to-have criteria.
- **Outreach personalization.** Draft tailored messages to passive candidates.
- **Interview scheduling.** Coordinate calendars across candidates and panels.
- **Interview prep.** Generate questions and briefs for hiring managers.
- **Candidate summaries.** Package notes and recommendations for debriefs.

## Top picks

### Paradox (Olivia)
Best for high-volume hiring with conversational screening and scheduling.

### HireEZ
Best for AI-driven sourcing across the open web and internal databases.

### Humanly
Best for interview intelligence and structured screening workflows.

### Custom build with LinkedIn + ATS APIs
Best for companies with strict hiring processes or privacy needs.

## How to choose

| Situation | Best choice |
|-----------|-------------|
| High-volume hourly hiring | Paradox |
| Sourcing at scale | HireEZ |
| Structured screening | Humanly |
| Custom privacy or workflow | Custom build |

## Key design decisions

- **Fairness.** Audit screening for bias regularly.
- **Transparency.** Candidates should know AI is involved.
- **Human decision.** Hiring decisions belong to people.
- **ATS integration.** The agent must work inside your existing workflow.
- **Outreach quality.** Bad AI recruiting messages hurt your employer brand.

## Honest limitations

- Agents can replicate historical hiring biases.
- They miss soft signals a good recruiter catches.
- Over-automation can feel impersonal to candidates.
- Compliance with employment law requires human oversight.

## Getting started

1. Start with one repetitive task, like scheduling or outreach drafting.
2. Define clear criteria for any screening automation.
3. Run a bias audit before going live.
4. Add a human review gate before any candidate is rejected or advanced.
5. Measure time-to-fill and candidate experience.
