# ElevenLabs: AI Voice

## What it is

ElevenLabs provides high-quality text-to-speech, voice cloning, and real-time conversational voice APIs. It is the most common choice for adding natural voice output to agents, apps, and content pipelines.

## When to use it

- Your agent needs to speak aloud or conduct voice conversations.
- You want consistent branded voice without recording voice actors.
- You are building podcasts, audiobooks, automated phone agents, or accessibility features.
- Latency under a few seconds is acceptable.

## What it does well

- **Voice quality.** Among the most natural-sounding TTS providers, especially for longer content.
- **Voice cloning.** Create a custom voice from a few minutes of audio.
- **Multilingual support.** Many languages and accents in a single model.
- **Conversational AI API.** Real-time back-and-forth voice with interruption handling.
- **Developer tooling.** Clean SDKs, streaming support, and project management UI.

## Honest limitations

- **Usage adds up.** Cheap at low volume, but high-traffic voice agents can get expensive quickly.
- **Latency.** Real-time is good but not instant; sub-second responses require optimization.
- **Voice misuse risk.** Cloned voices need strict controls to prevent abuse.
- **Not open source.** You are dependent on ElevenLabs' models, pricing, and availability.

## Pricing reality

- Free tier includes enough credits for prototyping.
- Paid plans start around $5/month and scale by character count or minutes.
- A voice agent handling 1,000 minutes of audio per month typically costs $50–$200.

## Best fit

Agents and products where voice interaction is a core experience, not a novelty. If you only need occasional audio, consider cheaper alternatives like OpenAI TTS or self-hosted Piper.

## Common integrations

- **OpenClaw / Hermes** agents with voice response skills.
- **Phone and IVR systems** using Twilio or similar.
- **Content pipelines** generating podcasts or narrated articles.
